# WhatsApp

ChatterMate connects through the **official WhatsApp Cloud API** from Meta — not an unofficial bridge — so your business number stays compliant and never risks a ban. Your AI agent answers customer messages, and a human can take over from the same inbox.

There are two ways to connect:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sign in with Facebook" icon="facebook">
    ChatterMate Cloud. Embedded Signup onboards your number under ChatterMate's Meta app — no app of your own, no tokens.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Enter credentials manually" icon="key">
    Self-hosted, or anyone using their own Meta app. Paste a phone number ID and access token.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
**On ChatterMate Cloud, signing in with Facebook is rolling out as Meta completes app review.** If the WhatsApp card doesn't offer it yet, use the manual path below with your own Meta app — that works today either way.
</Info>

## How WhatsApp messaging works

WhatsApp enforces a **24-hour customer service window**: you can reply freely for 24 hours after the customer's last message. Outside it you may only send **pre-approved message templates**. ChatterMate tracks the window per conversation and tells an agent when a template is required.

## Connect by signing in with Facebook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the WhatsApp card">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and click **Connect** on the **WhatsApp** card, then **Continue with Facebook**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/whatsapp-connect-facebook.webp" alt="The Connect WhatsApp dialog offering Continue with Facebook" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete Embedded Signup">
    Meta's signup opens in a popup. Choose your business portfolio, then the WhatsApp Business Account and phone number you want to connect. If you don't have a number yet, you can add one here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign an AI agent">
    Choose the agent that answers this number and click **Assign agent**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/whatsapp-assign-agent.webp" alt="Choosing which AI agent answers the connected WhatsApp number" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
ChatterMate verifies that the number belongs to the account you just granted, registers it for the Cloud API, and subscribes the webhooks for you.
</Note>

## Connect with your own Meta app

Use this if you self-host, or would rather keep the integration under your own Meta app. All three Meta channels share **one** app, so if you've already set one up for Messenger or Instagram, skip to step 2.

### Step 1 — Set up a Meta app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Business app">
    Go to [developers.facebook.com](https://developers.facebook.com) → **My Apps → Create App** → **Business**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Business Verification">
    In [business.facebook.com](https://business.facebook.com) → **Business Settings → Security Center**, submit your legal business details and a verification document, and verify your domain. This gates production access and takes a few days to about two weeks — start it first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the WhatsApp product">
    In the app dashboard, add the **WhatsApp** product. Meta gives you a test number immediately, so you can build before approval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the webhook">
    Under **WhatsApp → Configuration**, set the callback URL to `https://<your-domain>/api/v1/webhooks/meta` and the verify token to your `META_WEBHOOK_VERIFY_TOKEN`. Subscribe to the **messages** field.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2 — Copy your credentials

From **WhatsApp → API Setup**:

| Credential | Where to find it |
|---|---|
| **Phone number ID** | Shown next to your WhatsApp number — a numeric ID, not the phone number itself. |
| **Access token** | Generate a **permanent** token via a System User in Business Settings; temporary tokens expire in 24 hours. |
| **WhatsApp Business Account ID** *(optional)* | The WABA ID. Without it the number still sends and receives, but ChatterMate cannot manage templates. |

Open the **WhatsApp** card in **Settings → Integrations**, choose **Enter credentials manually instead**, and paste them. ChatterMate stores them encrypted and verifies the signature on every inbound webhook.

### Step 3 — App Review

A test number can only message people who have a role on your app. To reach real customers, submit **App Review** for:

- `whatsapp_business_messaging`
- `whatsapp_business_management`

Include a narrated **screencast** showing a customer messaging your number and ChatterMate replying. Also complete, per number:

- **Display name review** (Business Manager → WhatsApp Accounts → Phone Numbers)
- **Message template approval** for anything sent outside the 24-hour window — each template is reviewed individually

<Warning>
A Meta **test number** can only message recipients you have added and verified in **WhatsApp → API Setup**. If a send fails with a recipient error, that list is the first thing to check.
</Warning>

## Message templates

Templates reopen a conversation once the customer's 24-hour window has closed. You write them in **WhatsApp Manager**; once Meta approves one it appears in ChatterMate and your agents can send it. Open them from **Templates** on the WhatsApp card.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/whatsapp-templates.webp" alt="The WhatsApp templates dialog listing an approved template" />
</Frame>

Each template has a **category**, and the category decides where it can be used:

| Category | Reopen a conversation | Start a new one |
|---|---|---|
| **Utility** | Yes | Yes |
| **Authentication** | Yes | Yes |
| **Marketing** | Yes | No |

That is why a Marketing template appears under **Send template** in an existing conversation but not in **New conversation** — Meta only reliably delivers Utility and Authentication templates to someone who has not messaged you, and Marketing carries per-user caps.

## Test it

Message your connected number from WhatsApp — before app review, from a number with a role on the app; after, from any number.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/whatsapp-customer-chat.webp" alt="A customer messaging the business on WhatsApp and the AI agent replying" />
</Frame>

The AI agent replies, and the conversation appears in **Conversations** with a **WhatsApp** badge, the customer's phone number, and full human handover.

<Tip>
Nothing arriving in the inbox? Check that the number you messaged is the one you connected — a WhatsApp Business Account can hold several numbers, and ChatterMate only answers for the one you selected.
</Tip>
