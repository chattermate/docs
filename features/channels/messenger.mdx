# Messenger

Connect a Facebook **Page** and your AI agent answers the direct messages people send it on Messenger. Replies land in the same **Conversations** inbox as every other channel, and a human can take over at any point.

There are two ways to connect, depending on how you run ChatterMate:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sign in with Facebook" icon="facebook">
    ChatterMate Cloud. Pick your Page from a list — no Meta app, no tokens.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Enter credentials manually" icon="key">
    Self-hosted, or anyone using their own Meta app. Paste a Page ID and access token.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
**On ChatterMate Cloud, signing in with Facebook is rolling out as Meta completes app review.** If the Messenger card doesn't offer it yet, use the manual path below with your own Meta app — that works today either way.
</Info>

## How Messenger messaging works

Messages arrive as **PSIDs** (Page-scoped user IDs), so the same person has a different ID for every Page they message. Messenger has a **24-hour standard messaging window**: you can reply freely for 24 hours after the customer's last message, and outside it only specific message tags are allowed.

## Connect by signing in with Facebook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Messenger card">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and click **Connect** on the **Messenger** card, then **Continue with Facebook**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/messenger-connect-facebook.webp" alt="The Connect Messenger dialog offering Continue with Facebook" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant your Page">
    A Facebook window opens. Sign in as someone who administers the Page and tick the Page you want ChatterMate to answer for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the Page">
    If you administer more than one Page, ChatterMate lists them so you can pick. With a single Page this step is skipped.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/messenger-choose-page.webp" alt="Choosing which Facebook Page to connect to ChatterMate" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign an AI agent">
    Choose the agent that answers this Page and click **Assign agent**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/messenger-assign-agent.webp" alt="Choosing which AI agent answers the connected Page" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
ChatterMate subscribes the Page to the messaging webhooks for you, so there is nothing to configure in the Meta dashboard.
</Note>

## Connect with your own Meta app

Use this if you self-host, or if you'd rather keep the integration under your own Meta app.

### Step 1 — Set up a Meta app

If you already created a Meta app for WhatsApp or Instagram, skip to step 2 — Messenger uses the same app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Business app">
    Go to [developers.facebook.com](https://developers.facebook.com) → **My Apps → Create App** → **Business**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Business Verification">
    Complete Business Verification in [business.facebook.com](https://business.facebook.com) → **Security Center**. It takes a few days to about two weeks, so start it first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Messenger product">
    Add the **Messenger** product in the app dashboard and link your Facebook **Page**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the webhook">
    Under **Messenger → Settings → Webhooks**, set the callback URL to `https://<your-domain>/api/v1/webhooks/meta` with your `META_WEBHOOK_VERIFY_TOKEN`, and subscribe your Page to the **messages** and **messaging_postbacks** fields.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2 — Copy your Page credentials

From **Messenger → Settings** in the app dashboard:

| Credential | Where to find it |
|---|---|
| **Facebook Page ID** | Your Page's numeric ID (Page → About, or the Messenger settings panel). |
| **Page access token** | Generate a Page access token for the linked Page. Use a System User token for one that doesn't expire. |

### Step 3 — Enter them in ChatterMate

Open the **Messenger** card in **Settings → Integrations**, choose **Enter credentials manually instead**, and paste both values. ChatterMate encrypts them at rest and verifies the signature on every inbound webhook.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/messenger-manual-credentials.webp" alt="Entering a Facebook Page ID and Page access token in ChatterMate" />
</Frame>

Then choose the AI agent that answers this Page, exactly as in the last step above.

### Step 4 — App Review

To message people who don't have a role on your Meta app, submit **App Review** for:

- `pages_messaging`
- `pages_manage_metadata`
- `pages_show_list`
- `business_management`

Include a narrated **screencast** showing a customer messaging your Page and ChatterMate replying. Reviewers reject submissions whose video doesn't demonstrate the permission being requested.

## Test it

Message your Page from Facebook — before app review, from an account with a role on the app; after, from any account.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/messenger-customer-chat.webp" alt="A customer messaging the Page on Facebook and the AI agent replying" />
</Frame>

The AI agent replies in Messenger, and the conversation appears in **Conversations** with a **Messenger** badge, the customer's real name, and full human handover.

<Tip>
Changing which AI agent answers a channel starts a fresh conversation for the next inbound message, so the new agent replies from its own knowledge rather than continuing the previous agent's thread.
</Tip>
